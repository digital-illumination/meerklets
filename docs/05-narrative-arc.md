# Narrative Arc

## Structure

The story unfolds in **5 acts**, each representing a stage of engineering transformation. The factory physically grows and evolves with each act. The narrative can be experienced as a guided tour, scrubbed with a time slider, or (eventually) reflected live from real data.

### Phase Mapping

The 5 acts map to **three narrative phases** — the factory's evolution from artisan workshop to autonomous system:

| Phase | Name | Acts | Floors | Vibe |
|-------|------|------|--------|------|
| **Phase 1** | The Artisan Forge | Act 1 | 1 floor | High-energy but chaotic. Manual craftsmanship. Constrained. |
| **Phase 2** | The Hybrid Assembly Line | Acts 2–3 | 2–3 floors | Industrial, "beta testing." Exciting but uncertain. Barriers. |
| **Phase 3** | The Autonomous Factory | Acts 4–5 | 4 floors | Zen-like efficiency. The factory as a breathing organism. |

### Camera Style by Phase

Each phase has a distinct cinematic feel that reinforces the narrative emotion:

| Phase | Camera Style | Why |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Phase 1 | **Tight, shaky "handheld"** — close on the manual labour | Intimacy, urgency, constraint. The audience is IN the struggle |
| Phase 2 | **Smooth lateral tracking** — moving left-to-right along the conveyor | Industrial process. The camera follows the work. Organised but still uncertain |
| Phase 3 | **Wide, majestic "dolly back"** — revealing the entire factory as one system | Scale, calm, achievement. The audience sees the complete picture |

### Code Block Evolution

A subtle visual thread runs through all phases — the quality of the code blocks themselves tells the story:

| Phase | Code Blocks | Detail |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Phase 1 | **Irregular, rough, imperfect** | Hand-forged on Code Anvils. Lopsided, dim, rough-edged — like hand-blown glass or rough-forged metal |
| Phase 2 | **Improving, more uniform** | Machine-assembled but still inconsistent. Getting cleaner as the process matures |
| Phase 3 | **Perfect, uniform, pristine** | Flawless geometric shapes, bright golden-white glow. Machine precision at 3x speed. Like perfect crystals |

## Act 1 — "The Workshop"

> **Phase 1: The Artisan Forge** — Manual era. No agents, no automation.

**Factory State**: 1 floor (open workspace, no factory yet)
**Metric**: `Human Code: 100% | Agent Code: 0%`
**Duration in guided tour**: ~45 seconds
**Camera**: Tight, handheld. Close on faces, hands, Code Anvils.

### Scene Description
A single open floor. No factory infrastructure — just **Code Anvils** and workstations. Aleksandr, Sergei, and Oleg sit at individual glowing forges, manually hammering out code. Every line of output comes from their hands. The code blocks they produce are **rough, irregular, imperfect** — hand-crafted artefacts, not machine-precision.

### Key Visual Details
- **Code Anvils**: Glowing desk-shaped forges where meerkats manually hammer out code blocks. Holographic keyboards project onto the surface. The forging metaphor makes the manual effort visceral
- **Irregular code blocks**: The output piling up on surfaces is rough-edged, lopsided, dim — artisan quality, not machine precision
- The room feels **busy but constrained** — lots of effort, limited output
- Sergei is **buried under a mountain of paperwork** (reference the paperwork image) — manual processes, documentation, reviews all piling up
- Aleksandr is at his desk but **looking frustrated** — he can see the bottleneck
- Oleg watches the older two, not yet sure what to do
- Coffee cups, sticky notes, printed documentation scattered everywhere
- Screens show individual files being edited — small, isolated work
- A **single output pipe** on the wall produces a trickle of completed work
- **Frosted glass silo walls** divide parts of the room — meerkats must walk all the way around them to hand work to each other. Data particles bounce off the glass

### Visual Tone
Warm, intimate, but clearly **insufficient**. The audience should feel the constraint — talented people, working hard, but hitting a ceiling. The handheld camera keeps us close to the struggle.

### Narrative Text
> *"This is how we've always built. One engineer, one keyboard, one line at a time. Every feature, every fix, every test — handcrafted. It works. But it doesn't scale."*

### Transition to Act 2
Aleksandr stands up, looks at the ceiling, and has an idea. He sketches something on a whiteboard — a rough drawing of a small robotic meerkat. Sergei looks up from his paperwork, intrigued.

---

## Act 2 — "The First Agents"

> **Phase 2: The Hybrid Assembly Line** begins — the birth of Meerklets.

**Factory State**: 2 floors (Floor 1 Infrastructure + Floor 2 Agent Factory)
**Metric**: `Human Code: 80% | Agent Code: 20%`
**Duration in guided tour**: ~60 seconds
**Camera**: Transitions from handheld to smooth tracking. The camera begins to follow the conveyor belt left-to-right.

### Scene Description
The building expands. Floor 1 (Infrastructure) is laid as foundation below — server racks being installed, data pipelines being connected. The original workspace transforms into Floor 2 — Code Anvils are cleared away, the first assembly station appears. The camera shifts from tight handheld to a smoother tracking style as the process becomes more systematic.

### Key Visual Details
- **Construction montage**: The building literally grows upward. Walls extend, a new floor appears below
- Floor 2 gets its first **assembly line** — a simple conveyor belt with one robotic arm. The Code Anvils are pushed to the side, not yet removed
- The **first Meerklet** rolls off the line — small, tentative, wearing a yellow vest (Builder). THE key moment of Phase 2
- It sits at a workstation and starts typing. The output is hesitant at first, then faster. Its code blocks are **more uniform** than the hand-forged ones from Act 1
- Aleksandr and Sergei **watch, coach, correct** — they're teaching the first agent
- Oleg immediately bonds with the Meerklet — no hesitation
- Floor 1 is being set up below — basic server racks, first data pipes glowing to life. Orange-vested Meerklets pull their first levers
- The **chimney** is installed on the side of the building — its first small holographic puff rises (sputtering, mixed soot and blue code symbols)
- The **floor number "1"** appears in stencil on the exterior, then "2" above it

### Visual Tone
Exciting, experimental. Sparks of neon among the still-manual workspace. Hope mixed with uncertainty. The smooth tracking camera gives a sense of process emerging from chaos.

### Narrative Text
> *"What if we didn't write the code ourselves? What if we built something that could? The first agent was simple — a single Builder. It was slow, it made mistakes. But it learned. And it was just the beginning."*

### Transition to Act 3
More Meerklets start rolling off the line. The factory is working. Then... things start going wrong.

---

## Act 3 — "Growing Pains"

> **Phase 2 continues** — the factory faces its barriers.

**Factory State**: 2–3 floors (encountering obstacles, Floor 3 emerging)
**Metric**: `Human Code: 60% | Agent Code: 40%` (fluctuates during barriers)
**Duration in guided tour**: ~90 seconds (longest act — the dramatic core)
**Camera**: Lateral tracking disrupted — the camera stutters and jerks when barriers hit, smooths out as each resolves.

### Scene Description
The factory hits **barriers to execution** — real-world obstacles that every engineering team faces during transformation. These manifest as physical disruptions to the factory. The chimney output fluctuates with each barrier — soot returns during problems, clears during resolutions.

### Barrier Sequence

Each barrier follows the pattern: **Problem appears → Factory disrupted → Team diagnoses → Solution implemented → Factory improved**

#### Barrier 1: Poor Documentation — "The Static Screens"
**Problem**: The conveyor belt keeps **jamming** because the "Blueprint" screens at each workstation are showing **static** — garbled noise instead of clear specs. Meerklets stare at the flickering screens, confused.
**Disruption**: Conveyor stutters and stops. Malformed outputs pile up. Meerklets bump into each other trying to work from memory. Some build the wrong things entirely. Code blocks produced during the outage are **even more irregular than Phase 1's hand-forged ones**.
**Diagnosis**: Sergei notices the static, traces it back to the knowledge source — there's no structured documentation feeding the screens.
**Resolution**: Sergei builds a **knowledge base station** — a glowing holographic repository. He feeds structured documentation into it. The static screens **resolve into crisp, searchable holographic blueprints**. Meerklets begin querying the knowledge base in real-time.
**Improvement**: Blueprint screens across the floor now show live documentation. Knowledge base becomes a core Floor 2 fixture.

#### Barrier 2: Approval Chokepoints — "The Laser Grid"
**Problem**: A **glowing red laser grid** descends across the main conveyor path. Code blocks and packages pile up behind it. The grid only opens when a Manager Meerkat physically comes down and presses a large **"COMPLY" button** on the wall — guarded by a flip-up safety cover, glowing green when ready.
**Disruption**: Massive backlog behind the laser grid. Meerklets stand idle, tapping their feet. The Manager Meerkat is busy and slow to respond. Brief opening, a few items pass, then it closes again.
**Diagnosis**: Aleksandr sees the queue building from his desk, realises every item is waiting for manual sign-off regardless of complexity or risk.
**Resolution**: Aleksandr replaces the laser grid with an **automated scanning archway** — a sleek gate that validates work against pre-set criteria. Items that pass flow through instantly with a green pulse. Only genuine edge cases trigger an amber alert.
**Improvement**: Automated approval gate as permanent fixture. Throughput increases dramatically. Humans handle only genuine exceptions.

#### Barrier 3: Communication Silos — "The Frosted Walls"
**Problem**: **Frosted glass walls** rise between stations on the factory floor, dividing it into isolated sections. You can see the silhouettes of Meerklets on the other side, but **data particles bounce off the glass** — nothing gets through. (The silo walls from Phase 1 have returned and intensified.)
**Disruption**: Each section operates independently. Data that needs to flow between stations ricochets off the walls. Meerklets on one side build something that already exists on the other. Duplicate outputs clutter the conveyor.
**Diagnosis**: Sergei walks the floor and realises teams can't see each other's work. He presses his hand against the frosted glass — it's cold, opaque, isolating.
**Resolution**: The team installs **cross-functional data pipes** that punch through the glass walls. As the pipes connect, the frosted glass **dissolves entirely** — the floor becomes one open, connected space. Data particles now flow freely between all stations.
**Improvement**: Open floor plan with visible data connections between all stations. Shared context is the default, not the exception.

### Floor 3 Emerges
During Act 3, **Floor 3 (Orchestration) begins construction**. As barriers are resolved, the meerkats start moving upward — from coding to designing. The first **blueprint beams** appear: vertical shafts of purple-magenta light transmitting designs from the emerging Floor 3 down to Floor 2's production line.

### Visual Tone
Tense, then triumphant. Each barrier creates visual chaos (static screens, red laser grids, bouncing particles), each resolution brings a satisfying restoration of flow — but the factory is now *better* than before.

### Narrative Text
> *"Transformation isn't a straight line. The old ways push back. Static screens left our agents blind. Approval gates choked the pipeline. Glass walls isolated teams from each other. But each obstacle, once solved, made the factory stronger. The barriers became breakthroughs."*

### Transition to Act 4
The factory has weathered its growing pains. It's now more resilient than before. The chimney output is clearing. Aleksandr looks up again — it's time to grow.

---

## Act 4 — "The Factory"

> **Phase 3: The Autonomous Factory** begins — agent-first.

**Factory State**: 3 floors (Floor 1 + Floor 2 + Floor 3 Orchestration)
**Metric**: `Human Code: 20% | Agent Code: 80%`
**Duration in guided tour**: ~60 seconds
**Camera**: Begins pulling back. Wider shots, steadier movement. The dolly back begins — revealing the system, not just individual floors.

### Scene Description
Floor 3 (Orchestration) is fully operational. The factory is now a real operation. The central holographic workflow display lights up for the first time. Meerklets are numerous — dozens working across all three floors. **Blueprint beaming** is now constant — purple-magenta shafts of light transmit designs from Floor 3's architects down to Floor 2's production line in real-time.

### Key Visual Details
- **Floor 3 fully active**: Orchestration equipment installed — dashboards, workflow stations, communication arrays. Magenta-vested Meerklets are the frantically busy **"Middle Managers"** — catching requirements from above, routing them to Floor 2 below
- **Blueprint beaming**: Constant vertical shafts of purple-magenta light between Floor 3 and Floor 2. Visible schematic patterns travel within the beams. The design-to-production pipeline is seamless
- **The central hologram**: A large, beautiful 3D node graph appears in the centre of Floor 3, showing interconnected agent workflows in real-time
- **Meerklet population explosion**: Dozens of Meerklets now visible across all floors. Different vest colours everywhere (orange, yellow, magenta). The Throng is forming
- **Coordinated flow**: For the first time, you can trace a complete path — request comes in → Architect designs workflow → Builders execute → Testers validate → Deployers ship → Monitors confirm → out the chimney
- **Code blocks improving**: The code blocks on Floor 2's conveyor are noticeably more uniform and brighter than Phase 1's irregular forged blocks. The machines produce better output than hands
- **Meerkats shift upward**: Aleksandr and Sergei spend less time on Floor 2 and more time on Floor 3, designing and reviewing rather than building. They are becoming **Architects, not coders**
- **Oleg** is on Floor 2, comfortable among the Meerklets, occasionally sending one up to Floor 3
- **The chimney** produces a steady, growing stream of holographic output — mostly blue/golden symbols, occasional red

### Visual Tone
Impressive, coordinated, satisfying. The factory feels **alive and purposeful**. The audience should feel: "This is working." The wider camera reveals the system as a connected whole.

### Narrative Text
> *"We don't write the code anymore. We design the factory that writes the code. Every request flows through the system — architected, built, tested, deployed, monitored — by agents we created. The engineers who once typed every line now orchestrate hundreds of agents."*

### Transition to Act 5
The factory hums. But there's one more step. The meerkats look at each other — it's time to move to the top.

---

## Act 5 — "The AI Factory"

> **Phase 3 complete** — the factory as a breathing organism.

**Factory State**: 4 floors (complete factory)
**Metric**: `Human Code: 0% | Agent Code: 100%`
**Duration in guided tour**: ~60 seconds + lingering final shot
**Camera**: Full dolly back. The camera pulls far back to reveal the complete factory — then keeps going. The final shot shows the factory small in a vast, optimistic landscape.

### Scene Description
The final floor is added. Floor 4 — the Human Command Centre. Aleksandr, Sergei, and Oleg take their positions. The factory below runs with zen-like efficiency — a **single breathing organism** that pulses with coordinated activity.

### Key Visual Details
- **Floor 4 construction**: The crown of the building. Panoramic windows installed, command chair placed, workbench set up, living wall grows
- **Aleksandr in his command chair**: Surrounded by **holographic world maps** and factory-wide KPIs. Not building — monitoring. Calm, strategic, satisfied. His vision realised
- **Sergei at his workbench**: Finally free from paperwork. Now he fine-tunes with surgical precision, makes improvements, responds to the occasional escalation from Floor 3. A cup of tea beside him
- **Oleg at his learning station**: Experimenting with Meerklets naturally — for him, the factory has always been here. The next generation
- **Self-organising factory**: Meerklets on Floors 1-3 coordinate without direct instruction. They self-correct, self-improve, self-scale. Floor 3's magenta Meerklets frantically route work as "Middle Managers" while Floor 2's production line runs at **3x speed** producing **perfect, pristine code blocks**
- **Blueprint beaming at full capacity**: Continuous purple-magenta light shafts between all floors. The nervous system of the building
- **The chimney**: **Pure golden beam** at maximum power. Perfect engineering symbols (`{ }`, `=>`, `++`, `< />`, `fn`) shoot upward at high velocity. The vortex is tight, bright, constant. Easter eggs occasionally appear — meerkat silhouettes, CTM pound sign, Rewards icons
- **The factory pulses**: Subtle visual rhythm — the building breathes with activity. Each floor is a different colour band (orange, yellow, magenta, gold) visible through the cutaway
- **Business park exterior**: Other smaller factory buildings are starting to appear in the background — the model is spreading. Each has its own emerging chimney beam
- **Stencil numbers 1-4** clearly visible on the exterior wall

### Final Shot
Camera performs the **full dolly back** — pulling far away to reveal the complete factory in the business park. The chimney's golden beam rises into the golden hour sky. The building glows from within, each floor a different colour. In the far distance, other small factories are starting up. On Floor 4, through the panoramic windows, Aleksandr raises a glass. The factory hums. The camera keeps pulling back — the factory becomes small in a vast, peaceful, utopian landscape. The golden beam is a thin line rising into the sky. The end.

### Visual Tone
Triumphant, aspirational, zen-like. This is the destination. The audience should feel: "This is where we're going. This is possible." The wide, majestic camera reveals not just a factory, but a movement.

### Narrative Text
> *"We are no longer engineers who write code. We are engineers who build and maintain the factory. The factory writes the code. Every feature, every fix, every test — delivered by agents we designed, trained, and deployed. Our role hasn't diminished — it has evolved. We went from craftsmen to architects. From typing code to designing systems that create it. This is the AI Factory. Simples."*
