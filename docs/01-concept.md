# Concept & Vision

## The Idea

**Meerklets** is a 3D interactive visualisation of an AI Factory — a narrative-driven experience that shows how an engineering organisation (Product Engineering at Compare the Market) evolves from delivering solutions by writing 100% code manually to an **agent-first model**, where engineers build and maintain a factory that orchestrates AI agents ("Meerklets") to deliver 100% of the code.

## The Name

**Meerklets** = **Meerkats** + **Thronglets**

### Compare the Market Meerkats
Compare the Market's iconic brand characters — anthropomorphic Russian meerkats led by Aleksandr Orlov, his sidekick Sergei, and adopted baby Oleg. The brand has been running since 2009 and is one of the most recognised advertising campaigns in the UK. The characters have distinct personalities, wardrobes, and roles that map naturally onto an engineering team.

### Black Mirror: Thronglets
From the Black Mirror Season 7 episode *"Plaything"* (April 2025), written by Charlie Brooker, directed by David Slade. Thronglets are small, sentient digital creatures that live inside a 1990s PC game.

**Key parallels to Meerklets:**

| Thronglets | Meerklets |
|-----------|-----------|
| Small, cute digital creatures | Small, cute robotic meerkat agents |
| Born from a game, evolve through stages | Built in the factory, evolve as the factory grows |
| Learn from their environment and handlers | Learn from code patterns, documentation, best practices |
| Multiply rapidly (one becomes many) | Multiply as the factory scales — one agent becomes dozens |
| Form a collective ("the Throng") | Form a collective workforce (the factory) |
| Eventually surpass their creators in capability | Eventually produce 100% of the code output |
| "Small cute things with enormous hidden power" | Exactly this — tiny agents, massive output |

The naming is deliberate: it signals that these agents, while small and charming, are part of something far more powerful than they appear individually. The collective is greater than the sum of its parts.

### The Subtext
Charlie Brooker cited the 1996 game *Creatures* as inspiration for Thronglets — a game where you hatch and raise AI-driven Norns that learn, talk, and evolve. There's a direct lineage from those early digital life simulations to today's AI agents. Meerklets sits in that same lineage: the idea that we're creating digital workers that learn, adapt, and eventually operate autonomously.

## Core Message

> "We are no longer engineers who write code. We are engineers who build and maintain the factory. The factory writes the code."

The visualisation tells a transformation story in five acts:
1. Engineers write all the code (100% human)
2. Engineers build their first agents
3. The factory hits real-world obstacles and overcomes them
4. The factory scales, engineers shift to orchestration
5. The factory runs autonomously, engineers steer strategy

## Target Audiences

### Phase 1: Leadership & Exec
- First audience. Needs to understand the "why" and the "where we're going"
- Guided tour / story mode
- Visual impact and narrative clarity over technical depth
- Can be presented live or sent as a self-contained link

### Phase 2: Engineering Teams
- Deeper exploration mode
- Technical detail visible when zooming in
- Team-specific views showing their agents and metrics
- Motivational — shows what the future looks like

### Phase 3: Live Dashboard
- Connected to real adoption data
- Always-on display (wall screen, internal portal)
- The factory state reflects actual progress
- Historical playback to show journey over time
